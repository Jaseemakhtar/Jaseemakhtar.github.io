<About Me>

Assalamualaikum, I'm Jaseem Akhtar — a software engineer who loves building things that move, both on screens and in the real world. With over 5 years of professional experience in Android development, my journey slowly expanded into robotics, embedded systems, electronics, PCB design, and mathematical UI engineering.

I’ve always been fascinated by how ideas transform into reality using engineering. From childhood cardboard inventions to advanced software architectures I design today, the joy has always been in creating something from scratch.

One of the highlights of my engineering journey is the quadruped robot I built entirely from scratch — electronics, firmware, PCB, 3D design, inverse kinematics, and even a control UI developed using Android and Jetpack Compose. It started as a hobby project but evolved into a deeper learning experience in robotics, motion control, and system design.

In Android, I specialize in complex, mathematically advanced UI systems — custom canvas graphics, non-standard components, transitions, animations, and advanced Compose-driven rendering. My experience includes migrating legacy apps, building design systems, refactoring architectures, and designing pixel-perfect, high-performance screens for high-scale apps.

Over time, I realized that the common thread between Android, graphics, and robotics is mathematical thinking. Whether it’s a Bézier animation curve, a servo motion profile, or a coordinate transformation in Compose — everything follows the same principles of geometry, motion, structure, and logic. This realization shaped my engineering identity: someone who bridges UI + firmware + hardware + math.

Today, I’m aiming to transition into more research-driven roles in robotics and system engineering — places where creativity meets engineering rigor. I’m particularly passionate about real-time control systems, mechanical behavior, embedded optimization, and interactive graphics.
